Animals

Name Life Span Habitat
Sloth Depending on the species, sloths can live for about 10 to 20 years in the wild, though some may live longer in captivity. Sloths are primarily arboreal (tree-dwelling) mammals found in the rainforests of Central and South America. They spend most of their time hanging upside down from tree branches, feeding on leaves, shoots, and fruits.
Squirrel Tree-dwelling squirrels generally live for around 5 to 10 years, although some species may live longer in captivity. Squirrels are found in various habitats worldwide, including forests, woodlands, parks, and urban areas. They build nests, called dreys, in trees and use them as shelter.
Tree Frog The lifespan of tree frogs varies depending on the species, ranging from 2 to 5 years on average. Tree frogs are found in forests, woodlands, and other vegetated areas worldwide. They are adapted to climbing and can be found on trees, shrubs, and other vegetation near water bodies. They often lay their eggs in water or in moist environments.
Koala Koalas typically live for about 10 to 15 years in the wild, although some individuals have been known to live longer. Koalas are marsupials native to Australia and are primarily found in eucalypt forests and woodlands. They spend most of their time in trees, feeding almost exclusively on eucalyptus leaves.
